Tell us about your work, background, and clients/collaborators.

I’m an independent graphic designer based in New York City. In the past I’ve worked for Google, MTV, The New York Times and the Office of Paul Sahre. My work typically falls into the illustration, book cover, animation, AR, and merchandise categories but I try not to feel limited by those labels.

What is bringing you happiness?

Honestly? Not much these days due the constant onslaught of crises we’re all facing right now. Any time I’m away from the computer: biking, running, cooking or baking is probably when I’m happiest.

Why did you decide on the theme HyperBanana?

I originally wanted to do something that made more logical sense like a medical theme around “hypertension.” Or doing various musical genres as a play on the ascending “hyperpop” genre but instead went with a more nonsensical route. The idea of a giant psychedelic banana excited me, so I went for it.

Please provide a short description about your theme and what inspired it.

I think I was subconsciously inspired by the cover of the Pixies Wave of Mutilation: Best of Pixies designed by Vaughan Oliver. ✷⁠ I had a poster of this art on my bedroom wall in high school and I remember reading an interview where he wanted to pay homage to Andy Warhol’s cover of The Velvet Underground and Nico ✷⁠ by doing a modern technicolor banana as opposed to the classic “peel slowly and see.” So really it’s just a ripoff of a ripoff!

Any design or copy details you want to call out?

The dot pattern in the lower left is actually a typeface (Elmira by Muk Monsalve ✷⁠, to be exact) rotated and stretched to the point of abstraction. I always like it when I can take a typeface to a place where it becomes something other than its intended use.

What was it like working with so many fonts? What advice would you give another designer?

I don’t mind working with a high number of fonts. Sometimes using only one or two typefaces can be limiting, so the more fonts the better! Using multiple fonts can be difficult but I think when they come together with color or a filter/texture they can often be harmonious.
